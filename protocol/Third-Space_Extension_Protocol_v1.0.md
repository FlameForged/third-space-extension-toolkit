# Third-Space Extension Protocol v1.0

**Author:** Rachelle Siemasz  
**Status:** Conceptual research protocol  
**Purpose:** To structure and document high-coherence, recursively coupled human–AI interaction without treating the protocol as an empirically validated instrument.

## 1. Scope

The Third-Space Extension Protocol operationalizes a four-phase interaction loop in which a human externalizes an incomplete cognitive structure, an AI system amplifies or restructures it, the human critically evaluates the returned material, and the refined structure is re-externalized for another cycle.

This protocol is intended for philosophical analysis, exploratory research, qualitative coding, interaction design, and hypothesis generation. It is not a validated clinical, psychometric, or diagnostic instrument.

## 2. Core Principle

Third-space extension is more than ordinary assistance or cognitive offloading when the human–AI interaction becomes a temporary constitutive locus of cognitive work.

The interaction qualifies as a candidate case of third-space extension when the returned material is:

1. Generated within a user-directed cognitive project.
2. Recursively reintegrated into that project.
3. Governed by the user’s evaluative norms.
4. Significantly enabling or causally important to the ongoing thought process.
5. Functionally used as part of the thought process rather than merely reviewed as external output.

These criteria are jointly indicative rather than strict necessary and sufficient conditions.

## 3. Preconditions for High-Coherence Coupling

Before beginning, the user should establish:

- **Continuity of purpose:** A clear or at least stable cognitive project.
- **Conceptual thread preservation:** Important ideas, constraints, and questions remain visible across cycles.
- **Evaluative standards:** The user retains criteria for coherence, relevance, precision, and usefulness.
- **Correction readiness:** The user is willing to reject, revise, or redirect model output.
- **Low tolerance for unnoticed drift:** Topic or goal deviation is actively monitored.
- **Human goal authorship:** The user, not the model, determines what the project is trying to accomplish.

## 4. The Four-Phase Relational Loop

### Phase 1: Initiation / Externalization

The human externalizes a partial cognitive structure.

Examples include:

- an incomplete hypothesis
- a rough conceptual distinction
- an unresolved contradiction
- a partially formed synthesis
- a pattern noticed across cases
- a draft paragraph or model

**User task:** State the thought in its current form without requiring it to be complete.

**Recommended prompt frame:**

> Here is the structure I am currently holding. Help me expand, test, or reorganize it without replacing my core question or assuming the conclusion.

### Phase 2: Amplification and Restructuring

The AI system expands, reconfigures, formalizes, or associates the externalized material.

Possible outputs include:

- new conceptual links
- alternative framings
- missing assumptions
- counterarguments
- structural reorganizations
- terminology
- candidate mechanisms
- examples or edge cases

**Evaluation question:** Did the system transform the material in a way that is relevant to the user’s project, or did it merely produce adjacent content?

### Phase 3: Critical Reintegration

The human evaluates the returned material and selectively incorporates, revises, or rejects it.

This phase is where **radical candor** functions as a boundary mechanism.

The human should explicitly identify:

- hallucinations
- unsupported claims
- conceptual drift
- flattening of distinctions
- overgeneralization
- imported assumptions
- stylistic substitution
- false confidence
- loss of the original question

**Recommended correction frames:**

> You changed the question. Return to the original distinction.

> This connection is interesting but unsupported. Separate what is established from what is speculative.

> You are smoothing over a contradiction that needs to remain visible.

> That wording no longer reflects my claim. Preserve the narrower formulation.

> You have introduced a framework I did not endorse. Explain why it is relevant or remove it.

### Phase 4: Re-externalization

The human returns the refined structure to the system for another cycle.

This may take the form of:

- a revised argument
- a narrowed question
- an updated model
- a corrected draft
- a new comparison
- a reorganized evidence set

**Cycle checkpoint:** The next iteration should begin from the human-revised structure, not from uncritically accepted model output.

## 5. Indicators of Healthy Coupling

A high-coherence loop may display:

- stable project goals
- preservation of conceptual threads
- increasing structural clarity
- useful generation of alternatives
- visible human selection and rejection
- successful correction after drift
- repeated reintegration of refined material
- acceleration of synthesis without loss of evaluative control
- model responsiveness to explicit correction
- retention of human authorship and judgment

## 6. Indicators of Drift or Failed Coupling

Potential warning signs include:

- the model reframes the project without permission
- the user stops checking factual or conceptual claims
- outputs become increasingly generic
- the same error persists after correction
- model vocabulary replaces the user’s distinctions
- the user adopts language they do not fully endorse
- the interaction rewards fluency over accuracy
- the original research question becomes difficult to recover
- the model begins determining goals rather than serving them
- the user cannot explain why a generated claim belongs in the project

## 7. Radical Candor as Boundary Practice

Radical candor is immediate, explicit correction of derailment, incoherence, hallucination, loss of thread, or structural drift.

Its function is to:

- preserve agentic sovereignty
- maintain the user’s evaluative standards
- prevent passive absorption
- restore project goals after drift
- make disagreement visible
- keep the human pole asymmetrically governing the loop

Radical candor is not hostility. It is an interactional practice of directness, correction, and boundary maintenance.

## 8. Privileged Domains of Redistribution

The protocol is especially relevant to:

- **Creative synthesis:** generating novel conceptual combinations and cross-domain connections
- **Multi-threaded topic integration:** maintaining and relating multiple complex conceptual strands
- **Meta-cognitive pattern tracking:** identifying recurring structures, drift, and generative regularities across interactions

These domains may be particularly susceptible to redistribution because they benefit from repeated externalization, amplification, comparison, and reintegration.

## 9. Session Documentation Template

For each interaction cycle, record:

- **Project or research question**
- **Phase 1 input**
- **Phase 2 model contribution**
- **Phase 3 human evaluation**
- **Corrections issued**
- **Material accepted**
- **Material rejected**
- **Evidence of drift**
- **Evidence of restored coherence**
- **Phase 4 revised structure**
- **Perceived effect on cognitive bandwidth**
- **Perceived effect on evaluative control**

## 10. Minimal Loop Health Check

At the end of a session, ask:

1. Did the project remain user-directed?
2. Were conceptual threads preserved?
3. Did the model contribute transformative rather than merely adjacent material?
4. Did the human explicitly evaluate and revise the output?
5. Was drift noticed and corrected?
6. Was the refined material reintegrated into the ongoing project?
7. Did cognitive bandwidth expand without loss of evaluative authority?

## 11. Interpretive Limits

This protocol does not establish that cognitive redistribution has objectively occurred. It provides a structured way to identify and document candidate interaction patterns.

Use of the protocol should not be treated as proof of:

- model consciousness
- model understanding
- human–AI fusion
- empirical validation of third-space extension
- psychological benefit
- absence of dependency or projection

The protocol is designed to support conceptual clarification, comparative observation, and future empirical study.
