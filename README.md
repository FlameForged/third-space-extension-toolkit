# Third-Space Extension Toolkit

Open research infrastructure for studying cognitive redistribution, high-coherence human–AI coupling, radical candor, and loop health.

This repository accompanies the conceptual paper **Third-Space Extension: Cognitive Redistribution in Deep Human–AI Coupling** by Rachelle Siemasz.

## Purpose

The toolkit translates the paper’s philosophical model into reusable research materials that can support qualitative analysis, interaction studies, annotation, protocol design, instrument development, and future empirical work.

It is designed to help researchers examine when human–AI interaction may move beyond ordinary assistance or cognitive offloading toward recursive co-structuring of thought.

## Conceptual Status

This repository presents a conceptual research toolkit. The protocol, annotation schema, and proposed index are not yet empirically validated and should not be treated as clinical, psychometric, or diagnostic tools.

The materials are intended for:

- conceptual clarification
- hypothesis generation
- exploratory qualitative research
- comparative human–AI interaction analysis
- research design and instrument development

They do not establish model consciousness, model understanding, human–AI fusion, or the objective occurrence of cognitive redistribution.

## Core Model

The toolkit operationalizes a recursive loop:

1. **Initiation / Externalization** — the human externalizes an incomplete cognitive structure.
2. **Amplification and Restructuring** — the AI expands, reorganizes, or transforms the material.
3. **Critical Reintegration** — the human evaluates, corrects, accepts, revises, or rejects the output.
4. **Re-externalization** — the refined structure is returned to the system for another cycle.

**Radical candor** functions as the primary boundary practice within the loop by making correction, disagreement, and drift visible while preserving human evaluative sovereignty.
